Join census data to calculate covid rates

PART I. Download census data from NHGIS

  • This part is optional. If you want to follow along with downloading data from NHGIS:
    1. Download today's data.
    2. Go to NHGIS and create a login if you don't have one.
    3. Click Geographic Levels and the + under State, then submit.
    4. Click Years and check 2020 under Decennial Years, then submit.
    5. Click Topics and click the + next to Total Population, then submit.
    6. Click the + next to Race next to Total Population.
    7. Click the Continue button in the top right under Data Cart.
    8. Again, click the Continue button in the top right under Data Cart.
    9. Now you should see Review and Submit. Click Submit.
    10. You should see a table of extracts you have downloaded including the status of your current one. Once it is complete, click the Tables button under the Download Data column.
    11. I recommend opening the CSV in Excel and saving as an Excel file to avoid issues with ID fields.

    • PAUSE.

    >PART II. Normalize census data in QGIS

  • In QGIS, open the layer called states + covid.
  • In QGIS, open the data downloaded with NHGIS. If you weren't able to follow along, use the data in the folder called nhgis-total-population-2020.
  • Join the census spreadsheet to the state shapefile with Covid data already joined.
  • Using the Field Calculator, calculate a new field of Covid cases or deaths per 100,000 people.
  • Make a choropleth showing your new Covid statistic.
